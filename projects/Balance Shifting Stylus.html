Balance Shifting Stylus

Haptic feedback on styluses enriches human-device interactions. Past works focused on active haptic feedback, such as vibration, friction change, and magnetic attraction/repulsion. However, interactions that can be made possible by altering passive physical properties of the stylus have been underexplored. In this project, we investigate the novel interactions and effects on the users that could be brought by changing the balance of a stylus in real-time.

System Description

A gear motor carrying a weight load can be moved to any destined location inside a prototype stylus according to commands issued from a computer.

  • Next

A gear set and a case are finely 3D printed and assembled with a gear motor and a weight load to fit inside a barrel 1cm in radius. The motor is designed to move along with the load to maximize weight shift.

  • Next

An encoder is installed on the motor to track the exact amount of motor rotation in real-time. Then, according to data from the encoder and commands from the computer, a PID controller implemented on an Arduino Uno board controls the voltage fed to the motor via a motor driver board (TB6612FNG), ensuring prompt and accurate movement of the weight inside the stylus.
