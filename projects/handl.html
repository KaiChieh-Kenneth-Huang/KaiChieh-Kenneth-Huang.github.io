Handl

Sharing contact information made easier

Goal

Facilitate the process of exchanging contact information (e.g. phone number or social media) during or after a conversation, which may involve multiple steps and can be tedious.

Outcome

A smartphone app that streamline the exchange process. Its core functionality is to deliver oneâ€™s contact information (phone number, name, and/or social media info) via QR codes, which are scanned and collected by the other person. This approach has the advantage of being fast, requiring little human effort, and independence from internet/cellular network.

Interviews and Affinity Map

According to results from nine interviews, we performed affinity mapping as a team. According to the results of the affinity mapping, I identified the following design high level implications:

  • Design should keep the needs of both of the people involved in the contact exchange in mind
  • Design should support common social media exchange
  • Design should support saving phone number and contact name
  • Design could leverage traditional SMS/calls to save/send contact since people are accustomed to these methods
  • Design needs alternatives for network-less situations
  • Design should adapt to the user's context of use
  • Design should minimize having the user type
  • If design involves search, the search needs to be accurate

Domain and competitive research

To understand the domain and competition, I gathered information through the interviews we conducted and existing solutions online. I then organize the data, analyzed the strengths and weaknesses of various approaches, and identified value propositions and customer segments unaddressed by current solutions.
