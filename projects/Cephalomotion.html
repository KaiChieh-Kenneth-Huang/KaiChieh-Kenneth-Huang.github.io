Cephalomotion

In situations when our hands are busy, the head becomes a viable means of interacting with the computer. In this project, we explored ways the head can be used to control the computer. A multimodal webcam-based system was developed in this project.

  • System Description

System Description

The system comprises 4 modes: idle, document viewing, scrolling, and cursor control. How transitions between modes are made and what commands can be issued in each mode are depicted in the figure on the left.

  • Next

The system utilizes a modified third-party facial landmark detector to identify the coordinates of the user’s eyes and nose in picture frames captured from the webcam. These coordinates are used to identify the user’s intention in all four modes.

  • Next

In viewing mode, the velocity of the eyes and nose are filtered and analyzed to determine the head gesture performed. In cursor control mode, a speed adaptive filter which increases filter strength as the cursor speed decreases is used to make long-distance cursor transition prompt while retaining great stability around the point of interest. Eye winks are detected by capturing sudden changes in intensity of the pixels around the eye.
