Ungrounded Static Electricity Release

People living in dry climates use static dischargers to avoid static electric shocks. However, common static dischargers are generally inconvenient to carry around. Furthermore, these dischargers require that the user reach out to touch the device before touching anything. Discharger shoes are a good alternative solution to the problem, but they are quite expensive, prone to wear down, and limits customers' choice of shoe style. Therefore, most people choose to endure the shocks in daily lives and only use dischargers when absolutely necessary. The proposed concept is to apply the mechanism of lightning rods—to release charge into the thin air by means of inducting an extreme electric field with a sharp pin—on a ring to release the charge accumulated on the ring wearer.

System Description

The solution proposed is a ring with safely protected sharp pins installed.

  • Next

These sharp pins will release electric charge into the air whenever there is too much charge on the ring wearer. This phenomenon is known as corona discharge. On a conductor with static charge, points of great curvature have a stronger electric field. When strong enough, nearby air will be ionized and discharge occurs.

  • Next

Additionally, in case of any residual charge on the ring wearer not released through corona discharge, the pins on the ring will induce electric arcs (insulator breakdowns) whenever the ring is in close proximity to a conductor. When this occurs, the electric charge flows evenly through the skin in contact with the ring, causing little or no pain. Therefore, the wearer becomes less likely to be shocked by touching conductors with the hand on which the ring is worn.
